## A Work-Friendly Review: Brew & Beans Cafe, Hyderabad

### Wi-Fi Speed and Reliability

The single most important factor for remote workers is internet quality. Brew & Beans runs a dedicated 100 Mbps fiber connection with a separate network for customers — meaning your connection is not competing with the café's internal systems or the POS terminals.

In practical terms, this means:
- Video calls run without buffering
- File uploads and downloads complete at full speed
- The connection remains stable even during peak hours (10 AM–2 PM)
- No VPN issues (the router is properly configured)

The network password is displayed on every table — no hunting, no asking, no forgetting between visits.

### Power Outlets

Power outlet access is the second most common complaint remote workers have about cafés. At Brew & Beans, approximately 70% of seats have direct access to a power point. The remaining seats are within extension cord reach if you bring your own (many regulars do).

Every booth and the Community Table are fully powered. The Window Bar has two dedicated outlets for single-seat workers who don't want to juggle cables.

### Seating Comfort and Variety

Brew & Beans offers four distinct seating zones, each suited to different work styles:

**The Community Table (seats 8–10)**  
A long shared table with individual lighting panels overhead. Designed for solo workers who want ambient company without social obligation. Popular with freelancers and graduate students.

**Corner Booths (seats 2–4 per booth)**  
High-backed, semi-private booths with table lighting and power access on both sides. Ideal for one-on-one meetings, client calls (with headphones), or work sessions where you want to spread out documents.

**The Quiet Zone (seats 6)**  
Located at the back of the café, away from the kitchen and entrance. Deliberately lower ambient sound. No background music in this section. Favoured by developers, writers, and students working on exams.

**The Window Bar (single seats)**  
For people-watchers and those who work best with a view. Power outlets, good natural light, and a surface just wide enough for a laptop and a coffee cup.

### Noise Level

This is the area where many cafés — even well-intentioned ones — get it wrong. Too quiet and it feels sterile; too loud and concentration becomes impossible.

Brew & Beans maintains what regulars call the "café murmur" — soft background music at 60–65 decibels, conversational buzz from other customers, and the pleasant ambient sound of coffee being made. This is, according to multiple productivity studies, the **optimal noise level for creative and cognitive work**.

The Quiet Zone drops this further to approximately 50 decibels — comparable to a library with light activity.
