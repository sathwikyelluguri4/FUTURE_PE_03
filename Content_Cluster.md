## What is a Content Cluster?

A **content cluster** (also called a topic cluster or hub-and-spoke model) is a structured SEO content strategy where:

- One **Pillar Page** covers the broad topic comprehensively
- Multiple **Cluster Articles** cover specific subtopics in depth
- All cluster articles **link back to the pillar**, and the pillar **links to all clusters**
- This interconnected structure signals **topical authority** to Google

For a local café like Brew & Beans, this means Google will come to recognize the website as the most comprehensive, authoritative source of information about cafés in Hyderabad — leading to higher rankings across all related keywords.

## 📝 Anchor Text Guidelines

Using varied, natural anchor text is critical to avoid over-optimization penalties while still passing keyword relevance signals.

### For the Pillar Page (inbound anchors)

| Anchor Text | Type | Use Count |
|---|---|---|
| best cafe in Hyderabad | Exact match | 4–5 times across all cluster blogs |
| best cafe Hyderabad | Partial match | 1–2 times |
| Brew & Beans Cafe | Branded | 2–3 times |
| our main café guide | Generic | 1 time |

> ⚠️ **Rule:** Never use the same exact anchor text in every blog post. Vary between exact, partial, branded, and generic to maintain a natural link profile.

### For Cluster Articles (cross-linking)

| Target | Recommended Anchors |

| Supporting Blog 1 (Coffee Drinks) | best coffee drinks, coffee drinks guide, what to order at a cafe |

## ✅ Technical Checklist for Publication

Before publishing any blog post, verify:

- [ ] Meta title under 60 characters and contains focus keyword
- [ ] Meta description under 155 characters and contains focus keyword + CTA
- [ ] URL slug is lowercase, hyphenated, contains keyword
- [ ] H1 appears exactly once and contains focus keyword
- [ ] All internal links are active and point to correct URLs
- [ ] 2–3 real photos added before publication
- [ ] Blog submitted to Google Search Console after publishing
- [ ] Google Business Profile updated with a post linking to the new blog
- [ ] LocalBusiness schema markup present in page `<head>`
